Nick Mason

Nick Mason, is the band’s drummer and actually the only member to have remained in the group for its entire existence. He met his bandmates to be Roger and Rick while studying architecture at Regent Street Polytechnic.

Richard Wright

Richard Wright, known as Rick, was a founder member, keyboardist, and vocalist of the band. Rick was kicked out of the band by Roger while they were recording album “The Wall” in 1979. He was an integral member, though his creative input lessened over the years as Roger increasingly assumed control of the band.

Roger Waters

Roger Waters, known as the bassist, song writer and the co-founded the band. It has been said that he is the driving force behind albums such as “Dark side of the Moon”,“The Wall” and “Wish you were here”.

David Gilmour

David Glimour, internationally known for his incisive, atmospheric guitar work and vocals with Pink Floyd. He joined as guitarist and co-lead vocalist in 1968. shortly before the departure of founder member Syd Barrett. He even became the leader of the group.

Syd Barret

Founder and the original singer, songwriter and lead guitarist of Pink Floyd. He was responsible for their first and one of the best albums ever “The Piper at the Gates of Dawn”. It’s not know if he was fired or left the band early in the 1968. after erratic behaviour that made him too difficult to deal with.

Bob Klose

Bob Klose, was , between 1964 and July 1965, he lead guitarist of an early rock band that later became Pink Floyd. It is said that Syd Barret was the lead guitarist of the band, but Bob Klose was originally the first one that had become that. Members have said that his departure helped them to get away from blues and R&B, and make a way for something more original.
